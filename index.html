HOW TO USE THIS APP

  1. Upload your terrain CSV with columns x, y, z (all in metres, local coordinates).
  2. Set your domain centre (real-world lat/lon of the terrain's centre point).
  3. Set dx, dy (WRF grid spacing in metres) and e_we/e_sn (number of grid points). Recommended: start at 100–500 m, ≥100×100 points.
  4. Leave projection as Lambert conformal unless you have a reason to change it.
  5. Set your WPS_GEOG path to where you downloaded the mandatory geographic data on your machine.
  6. Click Generate WPS Files. The app reprojects your terrain, resamples it to the WRF grid, and builds all required files.
  7. Download the ZIP. It contains: namelist.wps, GEOGRID.TBL.patch, and a custom_hgt/ folder with the binary tile + index.

ON YOUR WRF MACHINE

  1. Copy namelist.wps into your WPS run directory.
  2. Copy custom_hgt/ folder into your WPS_GEOG/ directory.
  3. Append the GEOGRID.TBL.patch content to your geogrid/GEOGRID.TBL, replacing (or adding above) the existing HGT_M entry.
  4. Run ./geogrid.exe — output will be geo_em.d01.nc.
  5. Verify terrain: ncdump -v HGT_M geo_em.d01.nc | tail -30

IMPORTANT NOTES

  1. The binary tile is in WPS format: little-endian int16, row order bottom-to-top (south→north), scaled by 1 (integer metres). Elevations are rounded to the nearest metre.
  2. The terrain is projected assuming a flat-earth approximation around the centre point (equirectangular). For domains <50 km this is accurate to <0.1%. For larger domains, use a real DEM (SRTM) instead.
  3. The custom terrain only overrides HGT_M. All other fields (land use, soil, vegetation) still come from standard WPS_GEOG data.
  4. If your CSV covers less area than the WRF domain, missing cells are filled with the CSV minimum elevation.
